Privacy Policy

Pick Three for iOS · Effective June 11, 2026

Pick Three is built so that we — the developer — never see your data. This policy explains exactly what that means.

What we collect

Nothing. Pick Three has no accounts, no sign-up, no analytics, no advertising, no tracking, and no third-party SDKs that collect data. We do not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information. The App Store privacy label for Pick Three reads "Data Not Collected" because that is literally how the app is built.

Where your tasks live

  • On your device. Your tasks, descriptions, labels, and focus history are stored locally on your iPhone.
  • In your own iCloud. If you are signed into iCloud, the app syncs your tasks through your personal, private CloudKit database so they survive a lost or new phone. This data is encrypted in transit and at rest by Apple, lives inside your Apple ID, and is technically inaccessible to us. If you are not signed into iCloud, the app simply works on-device.

Purchases

Pick Three Plus subscriptions and the lifetime unlock are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store (StoreKit). We never see your payment details, name, or address. Purchase receipts are validated on your device.

Notifications

If you allow notifications, the app schedules them locally on your device (an optional daily reminder and optional one-time task nudges). No notification data leaves your phone; we do not use push servers.

What we can't do

Because there is no server and no account, we cannot read your tasks, identify you, build a profile of you, or hand your data to anyone — even if asked. Deleting the app (and, if you used iCloud sync, the app's data in Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Storage) removes everything.

Children

Pick Three does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children. The app is rated 4+.

Changes

If this policy ever changes (for example, if a future feature required a server), we will update this page and the date above before the change ships, and the App Store privacy label will be updated to match.
